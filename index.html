What You're Looking At
NERVA Globe is a signal intelligence surface built on quantum-inspired decision mathematics. Every bar rising from the globe is a NERVA node — a geopolitical actor, climate system, insurance market, financial hub, or local risk zone. The bar's height tells you how readable the signal is. The color tells you what decision the math recommends.
The key insight: You are not looking down at a map. You are standing outside the globe and every node is broadcasting a signal at you. Some signals are clean and actionable. Some are pure noise. NERVA tells you which is which.

Reading the Bars
Bar height is the inverse of entropy — the lower the entropy (more decision coherence), the taller the bar. A tall green bar means "clean signal, act here." A short red bar means "noise, stand aside." A short red bar with a purple ring means the node is chaotic now but trending toward commitment — that's the opportunity signal.
Hover any bar for an instant tooltip that explains its height in plain English before you click into the full analysis.

The Math (Plain English)
NERVA uses von Neumann entropy from quantum mechanics — a measurement of how mixed or uncertain a quantum state is. Applied to decision-making: high entropy = many equally plausible outcomes = noise. Low entropy = state has collapsed to a clear trajectory = signal.
Each node lives on a Bloch sphere — a sphere where the north pole is COMMIT (pure eigenstate, clean signal) and the equator is maximum uncertainty. The small Bloch diagram in each node's detail panel shows exactly where on that sphere the decision state sits.
The time horizon slider at the bottom shifts the entropy calculation across six time windows from 24 hours to 10 years. Watch what happens when you slide from flash crisis to civilizational scale — some nodes that are TOXIC now become COMMIT at 10 years. That transition is the opportunity surface.

The six time windows aren't just zoom levels on a timeline — each represents a fundamentally different type of decision surface, with different entropy physics governing what is readable and what is noise.

The inversion principle: A node that reads TOXIC at 24h but COMMIT at 10yr is not contradictory — it means the system is in a painful transition toward a locked state. Iran at 24h is maximum entropy war. At 10yr, some form of resolution (regime change, exhaustion, normalization) has collapsed the superposition. The gap between these states is where NERVA finds asymmetric value.
What Changes vs What Stays Fixed
Changes with horizon: Event-driven entropy, political volatility, financial noise, tactical military situations. These are high-frequency oscillations that decay out over time.
Stays fixed regardless of horizon: Geography (chokepoints, arable land), demographics (India's dividend is locked regardless of government), physics (Arctic ice melt trajectory, Cascadia subduction return period). These nodes read the same at 24h and 10yr because the underlying attractor is the same — what changes is only the entropy wrapping around them.
The most important 10-year signal: Find nodes that are ESCALATE or TOXIC at 24h but COMMIT at 10yr, AND where the gap is driven by physics/demographics rather than politics. These are the ones where the 10yr trajectory is nearly certain even though the path is volatile. That's where NERVA's opportunity layer pays off most — you can see the destination clearly even when the road is chaotic.
The ρ₀₁ term (coherence coupling) is the off-diagonal element of the density matrix. It measures how strongly a node is coupled to its adjacent decision field. High ρ₀₁ = the node is influential and connected. The geodesic arcs between nodes are weighted by ρ₀₁ decay — the arc collapses toward the globe surface as coherence is lost across distance.
The Opportunity Layer
The ◈ Opportunity Surface inverts NERVA's normal logic. Instead of flagging clean signals, it identifies nodes that are TOXIC or ESCALATE at the current horizon but trend toward COMMIT or HOLD at the 2-year horizon. This is the asymmetric entry calculation — maximum fear priced in now, trajectory improving later.
Opportunity Score (0–100) is computed from the entropy gap between current and 2-year states, amplified if the node has explicit contrarian logic attached. Each opportunity node in the detail panel shows the specific investment or decision thesis.
